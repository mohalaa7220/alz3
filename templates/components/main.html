Alzheimer's Disease (AD) Is A Neurodegenerative Disorder Characterized By Progressive Cognitive Decline And Memory Loss. Alzheimer's Disease Leads To Brain Shrinkage And Eventually The Death Of Brain Cells. It Is The Most Common Cause Of Dementia, Affecting Millions Of People Around The World. It Affects Memory, Behavior, And Social Skills. Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) Is A Transitional Stage Between Normal Aging And Alzheimer's Disease, In Which Individuals Experience Mild Cognitive Deficits That Do Not Significantly Interfere With Daily Activities. Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) Can Develop Into Alzheimer's Disease Or Other Forms Of Dementia.
